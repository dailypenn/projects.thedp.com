Black Wharton Undergraduate Association


Established in 1985, Black Wharton's been in the business of "killing the game" for 30 years. In fact, BWUA members have gone on to serve as higher-uppers in companies ranging from Morgan Stanley to Bad Boy Entertainment. When we're not Diddy-bopping in boardrooms, we're sculpting our members into leaders of tomorrow; whether it be at our monthly GBMs, or our award-winning HEMM Conference. Our supporters are some of the most influential move-makers on campus, so don't be surprised if you see Dean Garrett donning a BWUA shirt down Locust. There's no doubt that we'll win. Don't believe us? Just watch.

Penn Democrats


Penn Democrats is a political organization whose mission is to promote progressive political values on and off campus, through dialogue and action. Penn Democrats is a major campus hub for stimulating political discourse. We work closely with Democratic campaigns at all levels to support the election of progressive candidates to public office. Penn Democrats participates in political campaigns, conducts voter registration drives, seeks to stimulate discussion through issue-based activism, and hosts events with politicians such as Bill Clinton. While we normally implore you to vote for Democratic candidates, we humbly ask you to vote for us in this bracket!

Penn Dhamaka


Penn Dhamaka is Penn's premier all-male dance troupe. We specialize in South Asian fusion dance, including styles ranging from dynamic Bollywood and bhangra to powerful hip-hop and step. Not only do we hold an annual show and year-round performances in Philadelphia, but we also perform throughout the greater East Coast. So, why do we deserve your vote? We're a brotherhood that uses artistry and finesse to entertain you, while spreading South Asian culture. Watch us perform or watch our videos, and you're bound to catch the fervor! A vote for us will definitely put a smile on your face.

PennYo

Punch Bowl


The Punch Bowl was founded in 1899 by disenchanted rich Ivy Leaguers who established a tradition of excellence and scathing social satire we maintain to this day, though sadly the amount of yacht owners on staff has declined steadily since our founding. At more than a century old, the Punch Bowl has joked about everything from the DP and the Vietnam war, to Wharton and your mom. We hope you like the magazine and our website, and that you’ll laugh, cry, or be enlightened. Okay, maybe just laugh or cry.

The United Minorities Council has been the voice for minority groups on Penn's campus for over 30 years. We're all about promoting interculturalism and social justice here at Penn.
