FOOD

From boba to burgers, these are Penn students’ favorite places to eat and drink. Here are the Best of Penn 2021 winners for the category Food.

BEST BRUNCH

Sabrina’s Cafe

Sabrina’s Cafe, located near Drexel University, is known for its extensive brunch menu, including a tasty tofu scramble and multiple savory burger options. It also offers a variety of classic dishes, such as french toast, waffles, and eggs benedict, as well as vegan options.

ADDRESS: 227 N 34th St.

HOURS: Monday – Sunday: 8 a.m. – 3 p.m

BEST FIRST-YEAR DORM

The Quad

As one of the most recognizable landmarks on campus, the Quad has been the first destination for many Penn first years since 1895. Students living in the Quad are grouped into three college houses: Fisher-Hassenfeld, Ware, and Riepe. Known for providing the “first-year experience,” these college houses may be the perfect place for those looking to explore the social scene at Penn. The Quad will soon undergo a $200 million renovation, which is expected to be completed by 2027.

ADDRESS: 3700 Spruce St.

BEST PENN ATTRACTION

Ben Franklin Statue (College Hall)

Justus Clayton Strawbridge, of Strawbridge’s department stores, commissioned the Ben Franklin statue in 1896. It was sculpted by John J. Boyle and was unveiled in front of College Hall on Jan. 21, 1939 with University President Thomas Sovereign Gates on site. Legend says that The Button, located directly across from the statue, popped off the coat of this Ben Franklin statue.

BEST PENN ARCHITECTURE

Fisher Fine Arts Library

Fisher Fine Arts Library is one of the most recognizable buildings on the University of Pennsylvania’s campus. Designed by Frank Furness in the late 19th century, the red sandstone building is based on Venetian Gothic architecture. This beautiful building was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1972 and named a National Historic Landmark in 1985.
